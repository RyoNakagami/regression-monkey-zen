1. Why is your research question or hypothesis worth asking?

2. How is the current research lacking or falling short?

3. What gap in the existing research does your work aim to fill?

4. What impact will your research have on the discipline?

5. Will you be extending an area of knowledge, applying it to new contexts, solving a problem, testing a theory, or challenging an existing one?

6. Establish why your research is important by convincing your audience there is a gap.

7. What will be the outcome of your research contribution?

8. Demonstrate both your current level of knowledge and how the pursuit of your question or hypothesis will create a new understanding and generate new information.

9. Show how your research is innovative and original.
